Introduction To Chemistry

Chemistry is a branch of science that deals with the composition and properties of matter

Now, Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space. Matter is basically the foundation of everything in the vast universe.

There are three states of matter:

  1. Solid
  2. Liquid
  3. Gas.

States Of Matter:Solid

Particles of solids have no freedom of movement. They are closely packed together and can only vibrate in their fixed position.

Solids have the following properties:

  1. Volume: They have a fixed volume
  2. Shape: They have a definite shape and size which can only be changed by changes in temperature
  3. Expansion: On heating, they have a slight increase in size and this is due to the weakening of the attractive forces holding the particles together
  4. Contraction: On cooling, they have a slight decrease in size and this is due to the strengthening of the attractive forces holding the particles together.

States Of Matter:Liquid

Particles of liquids have little force of attraction which causes them to move about eachother. Liquids are called fluids because they posses the ability to flow.

Liquids have the following propertiees:

  1. Volume: They have a fixed volume
  2. Shape: They do not have a fixed shape. They take up the shape of any container in which they are poured
  3. Expansion: On heating, the particles of a liquid becomes more free and try to escape into the atmosphere as gas
  4. Contraction: On cooling, the particles of a liquid become closely packed together to form a solid.

States Of Matter:Gas

Particles of gases have no force of attraction which causes them to move haphazardly. Gases are also called fluids.

Gases have the following propertiees:

  1. Volume: Gease have no definite volume
  2. Shape: They do not have a fixed shape. They take up the shape of any container in which they are poured
  3. Expansion: On heating the particles become excited and move faster and more haphazardly which results in collisions against eachother and the walls of the container which increaces its volume
  4. Contraction: On cooling the particles become less excited hereby reducing the speed at which they move which creates a minute force of attraction turning it to a liquid.

Changes Of State Of Matter

Matter can change from one form to another.

Below are the different change of state of matter

  1. Melting: This is the change of state of matter form solid to liquid with an increase in temperature. The temperature needed for this change is known as MELTING POINT.
  2. Freezing: This is the change of state of matter from liquid to solid with a decrease in temperature. The temperature needed for this change is also known as MELTING POINT.
  3. Boiling: This is the change of state of matter from liquid to gas with an increase in temperature. The temperature needed for this change is known as BOILING POINT.
  4. Condensation: This is the cahnge of state of matter from gas to solid with a decrease in temperature. The temperature needed for this change is also known as BOILING POINT.
  5. Sublimation: This is an abnormal change of state of matter. It is the change of state of matter of a solid directly to gas and vice-versa without ever turning to a liquid.
